Behavioral Profiles

All axes normalized to [0–1]. Bluff Rate: dishonest plays / play turns. Bluff Stealth: uncaught bluffs / total bluffs. Doubt Rate: doubts / eligible turns. Doubt Accuracy: successful doubts / total doubts. Cards/Turn: avg cards per play turn (normalized by max).

Top-right = plays actively (bluffs and doubts a lot). Bottom-left = passive play. Most winning bots cluster in the middle.

💡 In Dubito, the winning formula combines selective bluffing (not too much, done stealthily) with accurate doubting (challenging only when suspicious). Blind aggression or blind trust both lose.
Strategy Analysis

Does bluffing more often lead to winning? An upward trend means aggression pays; a flat or downward trend means it backfires.

Sanity check: win rate and finish position should correlate strongly. Outliers here signal interesting edge cases.

Compare bluff rates across outcomes. If the Hard Win bar is consistently higher than the Loss bar, bluffing helps win.

Do bots doubt more accurately in games they win? A gap between Hard Win and Loss reveals whether doubt quality drives outcomes.

Do winners challenge more or less than they do in losing games? Selective doubters (lower doubt rate in wins) pick better spots.

Do winners play more cards per turn? Finishing fast requires playing large sets, but playing too aggressively invites challenges.

💡 Key takeaway: it is not how much you doubt that matters — it is how accurately. Doubting blindly forces opponents to pick up cards randomly, but missing doubts lets bluffers accumulate advantages.
